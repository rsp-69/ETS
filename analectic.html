Analectic Distribution

A fictional food distribution company demonstrating a real ERP-grade Product Lifecycle Management ecosystem — 62 branches, 5 channels, ~20,000 SKUs, 255+ customer groups — rebuilt from first principles in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Every tool here existed. Every methodology ran in production.

Tool 01 · Stage 1

Launchpad

The central control panel and Golden Thread entry point. Selecting a user and customer surfaced every relevant tool, report, and action for that account in one place — dynamically routing to the correct file path and silently logging every action taken, by whom, for which customer, and when.

Control Panel Dynamic Routing Silent Audit Logging PLM Entry Gate
Launchpad's silent logging was its most strategically important feature. Without interrupting any workflow, it captured who accessed which customer account, which tools were opened, and when — creating a forensic audit trail that followed every product through all six lifecycle stages. Anonymous forecasting was structurally impossible.
Tool 02 · Stage 2A

NPD Precision Engine

The mandatory demand gate for every new product entering the national network. Replaced arbitrary quantity requests with a customer-validated, store-level allocation model — enforcing Pipefill + 3 weeks of ongoing demand, synchronising all 62 branches in a single execution, with a time-locked 4-week review mechanism built in.

Demand Gate 62-Branch Sync Pipefill + 3wk Logic 4-Week Review Lock Digital Signature
No product entered the warehouse without passing through this gate. The Pipefill + 3 model accounted for initial launch stock, POS displays, BOGOF units, and customer-specific velocity — eliminating the Day 1 stockouts that had plagued every prior launch process. The 4-week review tab was time-locked — it only appeared after 28 days, forcing NAMs to reconcile forecast against real sales before the window closed.
Tool 03 · Stage 2B

Promo Forecast Engine

Uplift-driven promotional forecasting using 12-month historic sales as the baseline. Introduced customer-level sales share methodology, hub-satellite procurement modelling, and 4-4-5 financial calendar integration — keeping major accounts in sync with zero excess post-promo stock.

Uplift Forecasting Hub-Satellite Logic SOH/SOO Netting 4-4-5 Calendar Post-Promo Review
The hub-satellite logic was the most technically complex part of this engine. When a satellite branch sources from a hub, and that hub sources from a DC — the engine calculated each node's promo demand, stacked them correctly, and produced two numbers: what each satellite orders from its hub, and what each hub orders for itself plus its dependents. No other internal or external tool modelled this correctly.
Tool 04 · Stage 3

Item Maintenance & Compliance

The data integrity backbone of the PLM ecosystem. Governed the full SKU lifecycle — new items, deletions, reinstatements, range changes, and site loading validation — while solving the leading-zero problem that caused both the internal TOM project and external developers to produce corrupted data at scale.

SKU Lifecycle Gates Leading-Zero Preservation ERP Sync Site Loading Governance Ghost SKU Prevention
ERP systems strip leading zeros silently. A 6-digit item code like 012345 becomes 12345 — breaking every VLOOKUP chain, corrupting every customer mapping, and poisoning every diagnostic downstream. The TOM project failed to solve this with a full internal team and enterprise budget. External developers failed. This ecosystem solved it permanently, maintaining 100% data fidelity across ~20,000 SKUs.
Tool 05 · Stage 4

Logistics Investigator — Trident

Three linked but independent forensic audit tools sharing one data source, with a strict dependency chain — Prong 1 must run before Prongs 2 and 3 unlock. Together they assessed SLA window compliance, identified out-of-sequence deliveries for cost recovery, and validated customer order cut-off behaviour — building commercial dispute evidence automatically.

Dependency Chain SLA Window Audit Cost Recovery Cut-off Validation Dispute Evidence
Prong 1 established truth. Without it, Prongs 2 and 3 had no reference point — the dependency chain wasn't a limitation, it was a discipline. Out-of-sequence deliveries flagged by Prong 2 went directly into cost recovery conversations. Cut-off violations surfaced by Prong 3 resolved disputes before they escalated. The business always entered commercial conversations with evidence, never assumptions.
Tool 06 · Stage 4

Daily Lost Sales Engine

Captured a national inventory snapshot at 7:30 AM and automatically distributed 256 branch-level performance reports before 9:00 AM — zero manual intervention, every day. Embedded within: the Death Spiral diagnostic, detecting when short supply was permanently reducing future demand — a compounding failure invisible to every other tool in the business.

7:30 AM Snapshot 256 Auto-Reports TOS / TNA / NIR / DEL Death Spiral Detection Root-Cause Decomposition
TOS, TNA, NIR, DEL — four failure codes that decomposed every lost sale into its actual cause. TOS (Temp Out of Stock) is internal and fixable. TNA (Transferred Not Arrived) is a logistics failure. NIR (Not In Range) is a setup gap. DEL (Delisted) is lifecycle closure. Treating them the same is what made every prior SLA number meaningless. The Death Spiral caught the compounding pattern where each of these, left unresolved, silently fed the next.
Tool 07 · Stage 5

Weekly SLA & Service Level Engine

Measured performance across three layers simultaneously — PFD internal, vendor, and gross — so every failure could be attributed to the right source. Included a daily-to-weekly reconciliation layer, a financial impact converter (lost cartons to lost gross profit by SKU), and a customer-facing commentary report explaining what failed, why, and what was being done.

3-Layer SLA Internal vs Supplier GP Impact Layer Daily-Weekly Reconciliation Customer Commentary
Separating internal supply failures from supplier-caused stockouts made SLA measurement accurate and fair — removing the internal blame-shifting that had muddied every commercial conversation before. The financial impact layer gave leadership dollar-denominated evidence for contract negotiations. The customer commentary report replaced silence with transparency — reducing disputes by giving customers a clear explanation before they had to ask.
Tool 08 · Stage 5

Inventory Health Shield

Proactive runout projection engine flagging stock risk 3–4 weeks before ERP systems could detect it. Also identified excess stock, matched surpluses at one branch against shortfalls at another via site-to-site conversion, and automatically triggered post-promo clearance workflows — protecting working capital before write-off became inevitable.

3–4wk Runout Projection Excess Identification Site-to-Site Conversion Post-Promo Clearance Working Capital Protection
The site-to-site conversion turned a cost centre into a working capital tool. Excess at one branch matched against a shortfall at another meant one internal transfer replaced an emergency procurement order and prevented a potential write-off simultaneously. At scale across 62 branches, that logic ran continuously — before the ERP knew there was a problem to solve.
Tool 09 · Stage 5

Monthly Credit Claims Tracker

Two-layer credit analytics system built on a rolling financial-year QlikView extract. A white accumulation page built the historic record month by month. A black dashboard showed credit reason breakdown by branch with slicers for quarter, channel, and more. Filtering required both a populated credit reason AND a negative sales value — either condition alone produced systematically misleading results.

Rolling FY Extract Two-Layer Architecture Credit Reason Breakdown Dual-Condition Filter Branch & Quarter Slicers
The dual-condition filter was the intellectual core of this tool. A credit reason code alone means nothing — positive transactions carry reason codes too. A negative value alone could be a legitimate adjustment. Only when both conditions are present simultaneously is a true credit confirmed. Filtering on either condition alone silently inflated the numbers, misrepresenting branch performance in every prior version of this report.
Tool 10 · Stage 6

National Performance Dashboard

The strategic apex of the PLM ecosystem and the closure point of the Golden Thread. Consolidated forecasting accuracy, SLA outcomes, inventory health, lost sales diagnostics, and commercial accountability into a single executive view — with drill-down from national level to individual SKU. Built to support decisions, not monitor what had already happened.

TY vs LY vs Budget National → SKU Drill-down 5-Channel View Executive Reporting Golden Thread Closure
This dashboard closed the Golden Thread. Every product that entered through Launchpad in Stage 1 eventually appeared here in Stage 6 — its velocity, its SLA contribution, its inventory position, and its commercial outcome all visible in one place. The drill-down from national to individual SKU turned it from a summary report into a live diagnostic tool. Leadership could ask a question and find the answer without leaving the view.
